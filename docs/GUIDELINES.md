# Community Guidelines

## Code of Conduct

### Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone. These guidelines apply to all community spaces and interactions.

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not people. Assume good intent.
- **Be constructive.** When providing feedback, focus on how things can improve rather than just pointing out flaws.
- **Be inclusive.** Use welcoming language. Avoid jargon when simpler words will do. Help newcomers get oriented.
- **Be honest.** If you don't know something, say so. Accurate information is more valuable than a fast answer.
- **Be patient.** Not everyone has the same level of experience. What's obvious to you may be new to someone else.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Trolling or deliberately inflammatory remarks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Spam, self-promotion without context, or off-topic content in focused channels
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Communication Standards

### Asking Questions

- Search existing discussions before posting a new question.
- Provide context: what you're trying to do, what you've tried, and what happened.
- Include relevant code snippets, error messages, and environment details.
- Use descriptive titles so others can find and benefit from the answer.

### Providing Answers

- Be direct and factual. Correct misinformation respectfully.
- Link to relevant documentation or resources when helpful.
- If you're unsure, say so rather than guessing.

### Discussions and Proposals

- Stay on topic. Start a new thread for unrelated subjects.
- Summarize long discussions when contributing a new point.
- Acknowledge when someone changes your mind. It strengthens the community.

## Contribution Standards

### Code Contributions

- Follow the project's existing code style and conventions.
- Write clear commit messages that explain the "why" behind changes.
- Include tests for new functionality when applicable.
- Keep pull requests focused on a single concern.
- Respond to review feedback in a timely manner.

### Documentation Contributions

- Write for clarity. Assume the reader has basic development experience but may be new to the project.
- Keep documentation up to date when code changes.
- Include practical examples where possible.

### Bug Reports

- Include steps to reproduce the issue.
- Provide environment details (OS, language version, dependency versions).
- Describe expected behavior vs. actual behavior.
- Attach logs or screenshots when relevant.

## AI Safety and Ethics

As a community building with Claude and AI tools, we hold ourselves to an additional set of standards:

- **Do not build tools designed to deceive, manipulate, or harm users.**
- **Be transparent** about AI involvement in generated content or decisions.
- **Respect Anthropic's usage policies** and terms of service.
- **Report vulnerabilities** responsibly rather than exploiting them.
- **Consider the impact** of what you build. Just because something is technically possible doesn't mean it should be built.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation of these guidelines, report it to the maintainers through:

- Direct message to any maintainer
- Email to the designated contact (to be established)
- A private report through the platform's reporting tools

All reports will be handled confidentially.

### Consequences

Violations are addressed proportionally:

1. **Warning** - A private message explaining the violation and expected correction.
2. **Temporary restriction** - Temporary loss of participation privileges in community spaces.
3. **Permanent ban** - Removal from the community for severe or repeated violations.

Maintainers have the discretion to skip steps for severe violations.

### Appeals

Members who receive enforcement actions may appeal by contacting a different maintainer than the one who issued the action. Appeals are reviewed within 14 days.
