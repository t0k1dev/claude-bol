# Community Charter

## Mission

To build a collaborative community of developers who create, share, and advance tools and applications powered by Claude and the Anthropic API.

## Vision

A thriving ecosystem where developers of all experience levels can learn from each other, contribute meaningful projects, and push the boundaries of what's possible with Claude-based applications.

## Values

- **Openness** - We share knowledge freely, keep discussions transparent, and welcome diverse perspectives.
- **Quality** - We hold our work to high standards, write clean code, and provide thoughtful feedback.
- **Respect** - We treat every member with dignity regardless of background, skill level, or opinion.
- **Responsibility** - We build with safety and ethics in mind, considering the broader impact of AI-powered tools.
- **Collaboration** - We prefer working together over working in silos. Every contribution matters.

## Governance

### Structure

The community operates with a lightweight governance model designed to stay out of the way while ensuring accountability.

- **Maintainers** - Core contributors who have merge authority, set project direction, and make final decisions on disputes. Maintainers are appointed based on sustained, high-quality contributions.
- **Contributors** - Members who actively contribute code, documentation, bug reports, or reviews. Contributors can propose changes and participate in decision-making through discussion.
- **Members** - Anyone who joins the community. Members can participate in discussions, ask questions, and use community resources.

### Decision-Making

1. **Proposals** - Any member can propose changes by opening a discussion or pull request.
2. **Discussion** - Proposals are open for community feedback for a reasonable period (typically 7 days for significant changes).
3. **Consensus** - We aim for rough consensus. If consensus cannot be reached, maintainers make the final call.
4. **Transparency** - All decisions and their rationale are documented publicly.

### Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities | How to Achieve |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Maintainer | Review PRs, set direction, resolve disputes, manage releases | Invitation by existing maintainers based on track record |
| Contributor | Submit PRs, review code, report bugs, improve docs | Make your first accepted contribution |
| Member | Participate in discussions, ask questions, provide feedback | Join the community |

## Scope

This charter applies to all community spaces including:

- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Communication channels (Discord, Slack, forums, etc.)
- Community events and meetups
- Any other space operating under the community name

## Amendments

This charter can be amended through the standard decision-making process. Proposed changes to the charter require a discussion period of at least 14 days and approval from a majority of active maintainers.
